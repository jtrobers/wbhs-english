Formative

Activity Overview

This introductory activity prepares students to begin Long Way Down by learning about Jason Reynolds, exploring why the novel is written in verse, and examining the major ideas that shape the story.

Using a guided discussion organizer, students watch an author interview, analyze literary references, make predictions, and reflect on the themes they will encounter throughout the novel.

Learning Targets

  • I can identify important ideas from an author interview.
  • I can explain why an author might choose verse instead of prose.
  • I can make predictions about a novel using background information.
  • I can connect author's craft to meaning and reader experience.

Materials

  • Jason Reynolds introduction video
  • Introduction Video Discussion Guide PDF
  • Pencil or pen
  • Canvas assignment post, if used

Student Directions

  1. Read the brief introduction to Jason Reynolds and Long Way Down.
  2. Open the Discussion Guide PDF.
  3. Watch the Jason Reynolds interview.
  4. Complete Pages 1 and 2 (Front and back) while watching and noticing important ideas.
  5. Use your notes and reflections to prepare for class discussion.

Teacher Notes

Purpose

This lesson should do more than introduce the book. It should help students understand why the story is written in verse, how time and structure matter, and why Jason Reynolds wants readers to think about decision-making, trauma, being haunted, and interpretation. Flow should be - Introduction, Video, Guide, Pair Discuss, Whole-Class Debrief.

Anti-AI / Engagement Move

Use the printed guide on paper so students must respond during class while watching and discussing. This keeps the activity grounded in the interview and reduces generic AI-generated responses.

Discussion Starters

  • Why might verse be a better form for a story that happens in only 67 seconds?
  • What does it mean to be haunted by something that is not a ghost?
  • Why do difficult choices sometimes feel like crossroads?
  • What do you expect this novel to feel like based on Reynolds' interview?

Resources

Download or open the discussion guide used during the introduction activity. Students use this guide while viewing the Jason Reynolds interview and reflecting on key ideas before beginning the novel.
