Long Way Down
Floors 5 & 4

The investigation deepens: students examine Uncle Mark and Mikey Holloman while tracking turning points, mistaken assumptions, and consequences.

Formative

Activity Overview

In this reading activity, students continue the elevator investigation by examining Uncle Mark on Floor 5 and Mikey Holloman on Floor 4.

Students use the Elevator Case File organizer to identify important evidence, analyze how family stories shape Will's thinking, and explain how choices and misunderstandings can create lasting consequences.

Learning Targets

  • I can identify key details about Uncle Mark and Mikey Holloman.
  • I can explain how a character's choice or turning point creates consequences.
  • I can analyze how dialogue and events reveal character.
  • I can use one strong quote to explain an important idea from the text.
  • I can connect these floors to the module theme of choices and consequences.

Materials

  • Long Way Down novel
  • Elevator Case File organizer: Floors 5 & 4
  • Pencil or pen
  • Canvas assignment post, if used

Student Directions

  1. Review the question: How can one choice affect more than one person?
  2. Read Floors 5 and 4 of Long Way Down.
  3. Complete the Elevator Case File organizer for Uncle Mark and Mikey Holloman.
  4. For each character, choose one important quote and explain why it matters.
  5. Track the turning point, consequence, and lesson connected to each character's story.
  6. Discuss how these revelations affect Will's understanding of the Rules.

Teacher Notes

Purpose

This activity should help students see how the elevator ride reveals a family history of choices, grief, revenge, and consequences. Uncle Mark's story introduces a turning point connected to lost dreams, while Mikey Holloman's story reveals the danger of acting on assumptions.

Suggested Flow

  • Bell ringer: What is one choice that can change the direction of a person's life?
  • Read Floor 5 and complete the Uncle Mark side of the case file.
  • Discuss the broken camera, lost dream, and turning point.
  • Read Floor 4 and complete the Mikey Holloman side of the case file.
  • Discuss mistaken revenge and how this complicates Will's plan.
  • Exit reflection: What lesson should Will take from these two stories?

Discussion Starters

  • What dream did Uncle Mark have, and what changed the direction of his life?
  • How does Uncle Mark's story connect to the idea of a turning point?
  • What did Will believe about his father before Floor 4?
  • What truth does Mikey Holloman reveal?
  • How does Mikey's story challenge the idea that revenge brings justice?

Exit Ticket

Choose either Uncle Mark or Mikey Holloman. Explain one lesson Will should learn from that character's story. Use one specific detail or quote from the text.

Resources

Use the Elevator Case File organizer while reading Floors 5 and 4. The organizer will help students collect evidence, analyze turning points, and track how family history affects Will's thinking.
