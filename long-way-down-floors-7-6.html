Long Way Down
Floors 7 & 6

The Elevator Case File begins: students meet Buck and Dani while tracking evidence, grief, memory, and the first cracks in Will's plan.

Formative

Activity Overview

In this reading activity, students begin the elevator journey and track how Buck and Dani challenge Will's understanding of the Rules, revenge, and the gun in his waistband.

Students use an Elevator Case File organizer to collect evidence, make inferences, and discuss how Reynolds begins building tension and doubt.

Learning Targets

  • I can identify important details about Buck and Dani.
  • I can make inferences about how each character affects Will.
  • I can collect textual evidence to support a claim.
  • I can explain how Reynolds builds tension and uncertainty.

Materials

  • Long Way Down novel
  • Elevator Case File organizer
  • Pencil or pen
  • Canvas assignment post, if used

SOL Correlations

These correlations apply only to this activity: reading Floors 7 and 6, tracking Buck and Dani, collecting evidence, discussing inferences, and completing a short response.

11.DSR.1.C — Evidence and Inference

Students use textual evidence from the novel to support inferences about characters, conflict, and Will's decision-making.

11.RL.1.A — Literary Comprehension

Students demonstrate understanding of plot, character relationships, and key events from Floors 7 and 6.

11.RL.1.B — Theme Development

Students begin tracking how grief, revenge, memory, and the Rules contribute to emerging themes.

11.RL.2.B — Author's Craft and Style

Students examine how Reynolds uses dialogue, structure, pacing, and repetition to create tension and reveal character.

11.C.1.A — Collaborative Discussion

Students discuss evidence and build on classmates' ideas during partner or whole-class discussion.

11.W.1.D — Short Written Responses

Students complete a short evidence-based response explaining what one quote reveals about Will, the Rules, or the cycle of violence.

Student Directions

  1. Review the question: What influences the choices we make?
  2. Read Floors 7 and 6 of Long Way Down.
  3. Complete the Elevator Case File organizer for Buck and Dani.
  4. Choose one important quote and explain what it reveals.
  5. Discuss your evidence with a partner or small group.
  6. Complete the exit ticket before leaving class.

Teacher Notes

Purpose

This activity should move students beyond basic comprehension. The goal is for students to start treating the elevator ride like an investigation where each person reveals new evidence about Will, Shawn, the Rules, and the cycle of violence.

Suggested Flow

  • Bell ringer: What influences a difficult decision?
  • Read Floors 7 and 6.
  • Complete Elevator Case File organizer.
  • Pair discussion: What new evidence did Buck and Dani add?
  • Exit ticket: Choose one quote and explain what it reveals.

Discussion Starters

  • Why does Buck matter before Dani appears?
  • How does Dani change the emotional tone of the elevator ride?
  • What does Will begin to realize about the gun?
  • Where do the Rules begin to feel less certain?
  • What do Buck and Dani each make Will question?

Exit Ticket

Choose one quote from Floors 7 and 6. Explain what it reveals about Will, the Rules, or the cycle of violence.

Resources

Use the Elevator Case File organizer while reading Floors 7 and 6. The organizer will help you collect evidence, analyze character interactions, and track changes in Will's thinking.
